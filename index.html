Background

Research Ethics Review in The Medical City dates back 1997. This was the first established under the Chairmanship of Prof. Leonardo De Castro and was subsequently replace by Atty. Emmanuel Fernando. Its members, among others were Prof. Carmecita Loyola, Ms. Josefina Castillo, Dr. Mila Jocson, and Atty. De Leon. The Institutional Review Board then was under the stewardship of Dr. Mediadora Saniel, Dr. Felipe Estrella and Ms. Lourdes Lapuz in terms of managerial function. The IRB adheres to accepted international and local ethical principles involving research on human participants.

Accreditation and IRBognition of the Institutional Review Board is a priority of The Medical City. Policy Statement

The Medical City Institutional Review Board (TMC-IRB) was established to provide independent guidance, advice, competent review and free from bias decisions on health research or other specific research projects involving human subjects.

The membership of the TMC-IRB is multidisciplinary and multi-sector, as much as possible balanced in age and gender distribution, including the relevant expertise (i.e., medicine, research, social and behavioral science, law, philosophy, theology, environmental science, and public health).
